What is Photoacoustic Imaging?

Photoacoustic imaing (PAI) uses pulsed laseer light to generate sound waves. These thermoelastic expansions are facilitated by light-absorbing substances such as hemoglobin, melanin and lipids within the imaged medium. By detecting ultrasound waves, quantitative measurements of functional biomarkers such as regional oxygen saturation and hemoglobin concentrations can be measured deep within tissue (< 5 cm).

Quantitative Functional Information with Ultrasound Guidance

The use of ultrasound hardware allows simultaneous visualization of anatomical (standard ultrasound) and functional imaging (oxygenation mapping).

Our algorithms allows direct calculation of oxygenation from multiwavelength PAI data.
